# Update Profile Tagline: "ideate. innovate. iterate."

## Context

The current bio across the site reads: *"Ageless hobbyist. I love learning workflows and processes — then taking them apart to make them faster, leaner, and smarter."* — conversational, first-person, explanatory (25 words).

The new tagline — **"ideate. innovate. iterate."** — is a three-word manifesto: punchy, rhythmic, lowercase with deliberate periods. It shifts the voice from "approachable hobbyist" to "methodical builder." The three words actually map to the same themes (learning → ideate, building → innovate, optimizing → iterate) but at a higher level of abstraction that lets the site's projects and articles provide the specifics.

### Language Analysis

| Dimension | Current | New |
|---|---|---|
| Voice | First-person, warm | Declarative, impersonal |
| Length | 25 words | 3 words |
| Tone | Approachable hobbyist | Confident practitioner |
| Structure | Narrative sentences | Rhythmic triple motto |

**Alignment with site themes**: Strong. Ideate ↔ curiosity/AI exploration, Innovate ↔ building AI-first tools/projects, Iterate ↔ workflow optimization. The terminal/monospace aesthetic of the site actually suits a terse motto better than a conversational paragraph.

**Stats grid** (Focus: "Workflow Optimization" / Mode: "Perpetual Learner" / Stack: "AI-first Tooling") — keep as-is. They serve as the concrete detail layer beneath the abstract motto.
